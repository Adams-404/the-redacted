# THE REDACTED — Case Files

## Season 1: The Blackwood Dossier

### Episode 1: "The Loft"

---

### Part I — The Discovery

October 12, 2023. 08:47 AM.

Sarah Chen let herself into Loft 4B using her spare key — the third one she'd cut over the years for Richard Blackwood's various forgotten locks. He'd missed two board meetings in a row, which wasn't like him. Richard was many things — ruthless, paranoid, brilliant — but never absent.

She found him on the couch.

The television was still playing. A half-empty glass of scotch sat on the coffee table. Financial documents were spread across the dining table like a paper mosaic. The air smelled of whiskey and something else — something bitter, almost chemical.

Richard Blackwood, CEO of Blackwood Corp, was dead.

The door was locked from the inside with a chain. The windows were all sealed — painted shut years ago by a landlord who didn't believe in renovation. Except one. The bedroom window. Its lock was broken, the frame splintered. A single black fiber clung to the splintered wood, waving in the draft like a tiny flag.

Detective R. Parker arrived on scene at 09:15. He'd seen a lot of death in twenty-three years on the force, but this one felt different from the start. Something about the way the documents were arranged. Something about the glass — positioned just so on the coaster. Something about the dead man's expression, caught between surprise and something that almost looked like relief.

---

### Part II — The Evidence

The autopsy told the first lie.

**Time of death: October 11, 4:30 PM.**

Richard had been dead for over sixteen hours before Sarah found him. But Nadia Carter, the neighbor in 4C, swore she heard a loud thud from the loft at 10:12 PM — nearly six hours after he'd died.

*"I thought it was furniture,"* she told Parker. *"You know, someone moving things around. It was loud. A heavy kind of sound, like a body hitting the floor. But I didn't think much of it. People in this building are always rearranging their lives."*

The scotch glass tested positive for cyanide. Fast-acting. Lethal within minutes. Richard wouldn't have suffered long.

The toxicology report revealed something else: trace amounts of zolpidem — a common sleeping medication — in Richard's system. Not enough to kill him. Just enough to make him drowsy. To make him compliant.

Whoever did this didn't just poison him. They sat with him. They waited.

The black fiber from the windowsill was sent to the lab. It came back as a match for a high-end wool-cashmere blend — the kind used in custom-tailored suits. Designer labels. The kind of suit a CEO might wear. The kind of suit Richard Blackwood never wore — he was allergic to wool.

The broken lock told the second lie.

Forensic analysis showed the splintering pattern was wrong. The force was applied from the inside, not outside. Someone had broken the lock to make it look like a break-in. But the real entry point was never the window. The real entry point had a key.

And the only two keys to Loft 4B were on Richard's nightstand — and in Sarah Chen's purse.

---

### Part III — The People

Marcus Lowell was the first person Parker interviewed. CFO of Blackwood Corp. Forty-three years old. Drove a Porsche. Had a temper.

*"Richard was a dead weight,"* Lowell said, not bothering to hide his contempt. *"The company was bleeding money because of his terrible decisions. I tried to save us, but he blocked every restructuring effort. Every. Single. One."*

Lowell's office was a shrine to his own ambition. Photos with politicians. Framed awards. A letter opener on his desk that Parker noticed he kept picking up and putting down throughout the interview.

He had no alibi for the time of death. He claimed he was in a solo board meeting. No witnesses. No cameras.

But Lowell's suit — the one he wore to the office that day — was made of the same wool-cashmere blend found on the windowsill.

Parker asked him about it.

*"I own three of them,"* Lowell said. *"They're not rare. Half the executives in this city wear the same tailor."*

He was right. But it was still a thread.

---

Dylan Mercer was the second interview. Business partner. Old college friend of Richard's. They'd started Blackwood Corp together twenty years ago in a dorm room.

*"Richard was like a brother to me,"* Mercer said. His eyes were red. He looked like he hadn't slept. *"We were about to close the biggest deal of our lives. Mercer Industries was going to acquire a controlling stake. Richard was going to step down as CEO, take an advisory role, and finally retire. He was tired, Parker. He was so tired."*

The deal was worth $340 million. But Richard had been having second thoughts. Two days before his death, he'd called Mercer and told him he wanted to pull out.

*"I talked him down,"* Mercer said. *"I told him it was just cold feet. He agreed to meet me on the 12th to finalize the papers."*

The meeting never happened.

Mercer's alibi was solid. He was at a restaurant with twelve business associates from 4 PM to 7 PM on the day of the murder. The restaurant had cameras. The time stamps matched.

But here's the thing Parker couldn't shake: Mercer had everything to lose if the deal fell through. And Richard had been about to kill it.

---

Nadia Carter was the third interview. Neighbor in 4C. Twenty-nine years old. Worked from home as a graphic designer.

*"I didn't see anything,"* she said. *"I just heard the thud. It was 10:12 PM exactly because I looked at my clock. I was about to go to bed."*

Parker asked if she'd heard anything else that evening.

*"Footsteps. Around midnight. I thought it was Richard coming home late. He did that sometimes."*

Footsteps at midnight. Eight hours after Richard died.

Someone had a key. Someone came back.

---

Marcus Lowell's suit fiber was on the windowsill. But Marcus Lowell was at a charity gala from 8 PM to midnight with two hundred witnesses.

Someone else wore that suit. Or someone else broke that lock. Or both.

The math wasn't mathing.

---

### Part IV — The Truth (So Far)

Here's what Parker knew for certain:

**1.** Richard Blackwood was poisoned with cyanide at approximately 4:30 PM on October 11, 2023. The poison was in his scotch. He also had trace amounts of a sedative in his system, suggesting he was drowsy when he drank it — or was helped to drink it.

**2.** The loft was locked from the inside. The bedroom window lock was broken from the inside to simulate a break-in. The only two keys were accounted for — one on Richard's nightstand, one with Sarah Chen.

**3.** A black fiber from a high-end suit was found on the broken window lock. The fiber matched suits owned by Marcus Lowell, but Lowell had an airtight alibi from 8 PM onward.

**4.** Nadia Carter heard a thud at 10:12 PM — six hours after the estimated time of death.

**5.** Someone was in the loft at midnight. Footsteps. A visitor with a key.

**6.** Blackwood Corp was on the verge of bankruptcy. Richard was about to pull out of a $340 million deal with Mercer Industries. Marcus Lowell had been embezzling funds to cover personal debts.

**7.** The autopsy also found something Parker didn't expect: Richard Blackwood had been diagnosed with stage four pancreatic cancer. He had three months to live. He hadn't told anyone.

---

Here was the question that kept Parker up at night:

*If you had three months to live, and someone handed you a glass of scotch — would you drink it?*

*Or would you drink it if you knew what was in it?*
